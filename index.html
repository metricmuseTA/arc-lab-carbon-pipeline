The Problem

The public has been entirely left out of the conversation about AI's environmental impact. No plain-language tools exist for them. No independent source they can trust.

Invisible Impact

Billions of people use AI every day without any way to see what it costs the grid, the water supply, or the atmosphere.

Developer-Only Tools

Every existing tool that measures AI's carbon footprint is built for engineers and researchers — not the public.

Big Tech Opacity

The companies with the most data have the least incentive to share it. Independent measurement doesn't exist — yet.

No Public Accountability

Without visible data, there's no public pressure for change. The next AI race will be about energy — and the clock is running.

Our Approach

Start with real data, make it accessible in plain language, and expand the lens year over year — from carbon to energy to water to resources.

1

Start With Carbon

A live Bronze-to-Gold data pipeline using WattTime CAISO grid data — already built, already published. Real carbon intensity data, translated into plain language anyone can understand.

2

Expand the Lens

Phase by phase: energy mix (renewable vs. fossil by region), then water consumption, then full resource impact across all AI types. The public lab grows as the data grows.

3

Make It Real-Time

Move from historical analysis to live tracking — so anyone can see, in this moment, what AI is doing to the planet and when the best time to run workloads actually is.

4

Educate & Empower

Annual ARC LAB Environmental Impact Reports in plain language — free and public. Education as a catalyst, not just information delivery. Because when ordinary people can finally see AI's true environmental footprint, they become the force that drives the solution.

Three phases. Twelve months. One goal: make AI's environmental impact impossible to ignore.

About the Founder

Tiffani Anderson spent 17 years as an architectural design manager supporting Navy construction projects — shepherding $123M in military infrastructure across 6 installations in the Southwest, from RFP through congressional review, design, award, and construction.

In 2025 she walked away from that career to chase a belief: that data, used with intention, can change how we understand the world. After discovering a love of data engineering through Zach Wilson's bootcamp, she built and published a live carbon intensity pipeline as a capstone project — and ARC LAB was born.

ARC LAB is independent, public-facing, and based in La Mesa, California. All research is free to access. No corporate funding. No agenda. Just the data.
